Senate Preferences in Australian Senate Election 2013

This visualisation works by first grouping preference into group tickets. It does this for parties and also for independent groups.

It then works out the aveage position each ticket gives to candidates on each other ticket. If the average is in the top 25% of the preferences the we class this as being preferenced highly. If the party is preferenced highly they will have a thicker line pointing to them on the chart.

While this gives us a quick overview, in reality it's a lot more complex and the preference order is particularly important.

For a full list of all preferences I recommend belowtheline.org.au. Also have a look at this visualisation by Open Australia.

 

 

Visualisation using d3js by @peterneish, data from the Australian Electoral Commission, code on GitHub.
